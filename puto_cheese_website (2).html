What is Puto Cheese?

Puto is a type of steamed rice cake in Philippine cuisine, typically prepared plain or with various toppings and flavorings. Puto cheese, as the name suggests, is a delightful variation where cheese is incorporated into the rice cake batter or placed on top, adding a rich, savory, and sometimes slightly salty contrast to the sweet and fluffy puto.

In Davao del Sur, puto cheese has evolved with local flavors and preferences, becoming a beloved snack and delicacy. It's often enjoyed as a breakfast item, a midday snack, or even a dessert, paired with coffee or other beverages.

The Davao del Sur Twist

Davao del Sur's puto cheese stands out due to its unique preparation methods and the quality of local ingredients. Vendors often use freshly grated local cheeses, which melt beautifully into the warm, soft puto, creating an irresistible treat. Some local recipes might also incorporate coconut milk or pandan leaves for an added aromatic dimension.

The culinary scene in Davao del Sur embraces both tradition and innovation, and puto cheese is a prime example. While maintaining the classic steamed cake texture, local artisans experiment with different cheese types, savory fillings, and even sweet glazes to cater to diverse palates. It's not just food; it's a part of the local culture and hospitality.

Where to Find the Best Puto Cheese

When in Davao del Sur, you'll find puto cheese in local markets, street food stalls, and specialty bakeries. Each vendor often has their secret recipe, making it an adventure to try different versions. Don't miss the chance to experience this regional delight firsthand!

  • **Local Markets:** A great place to find traditional homemade puto cheese.
  • **Street Vendors:** Often offer freshly steamed, warm puto cheese.
  • **Bakeries & Cafes:** Modern takes on puto cheese, sometimes with gourmet ingredients.
